Substitutability between prime-age and marginal retirement-age workers (JMP)

Summary

Utilizing an internal migration shock, this study examines the substitutability between prime-age (ages 25-54) and marginal retirement-age (ages 55-64) workers. A 1% increase in young worker inflows boosts the relative employment of young to old by 1.47%, and reduces relative earnings by 0.19%, indicating an elasticity of substitution around 7.7. Based on the overlapping generations model, the utility of older workers has diminished in an aging society due to economic slowdown. However, the inflow shock can offset this decline by increasing total output and enabling older workers to earn higher wages. A high elasticity can further mitigate the reduction.

Keywords

  • Young-Old elasticity of substitution
  • Internal migration
