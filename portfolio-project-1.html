Substitutability between prime-age and marginal retirement-age workers (JMP)

Summary

This study examines the substitutability between prime-age (ages 25-54) and marginal retirement-age (ages 55-64) workers, utilizing the internal migration shock. In response to a 1% increase in young worker inflow, relative employment between young and old workers rises by 2.5%, and relative earnings decrease by 0.31%. Consequently, the elasticity of substitution between these two labor cohorts is approximately 8.

Using the local projection method, I reveal that the effect of the inflow persists until the subsequent year, with an elasticity exceeding 11. These findings suggest that a rapid and substantial level of substitution can transpire even within a shorter time frame when young workers flow into a local area. Moreover, in an economy characterized by such a high degree of substitutability, an unexpected inflow of young workers can significantly diminish the utility of older workers.

Keywords

  • Young-Old elasticity of substitution
  • Internal migration
